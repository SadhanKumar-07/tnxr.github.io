About Krishnagiri

Krishnagiri is a district in the northwestern part of Tamil Nadu, known for its rich history, mango cultivation, and granite industry. It shares borders with Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh, and serves as an important trade and transportation link between these states.

  • Headquarters: Krishnagiri
  • Area: Approximately 5,143 sq. km
  • Population: ~1,879,809 (2011 Census)
  • Number of Taluks: 5 (Krishnagiri, Hosur, Denkanikottai, Pochampalli, and Uthangarai)
  • Official Language: Tamil
Geography and Climate

Krishnagiri district is located in the northwestern part of Tamil Nadu and is part of the Eastern Ghats region. It features a mix of hills, plateaus, and valleys, making it distinct in terms of topography compared to coastal districts. The district experiences a moderate climate with hot summers and mild winters, and rainfall mainly occurs during the southwest and northeast monsoons.

Detailed Geography:

  • Terrain: Hilly and undulating, part of the Eastern Ghats; known for granite and mango orchards.
  • Rivers: Major rivers include the Ponnaiyar, Koundinya, and South Pennar which support agriculture and drinking water needs.
  • Forests: Rich in forest cover with reserved forest areas contributing to the district’s biodiversity.
  • Elevation: Ranges from 300 to 1,200 meters above sea level, especially in the Denkanikottai and Hosur taluks.

Detailed Climate:

  • Temperature: Summer temperatures range from 25°C to 38°C; winters can dip to around 13°C to 22°C.
  • Rainfall: Receives an average annual rainfall of around 850 mm to 900 mm.
  • Monsoon: Influenced by both southwest (June–September) and northeast (October–December) monsoons.
  • Hottest Months: April to June, with temperatures occasionally exceeding 38°C.
  • Coolest Months: December and January, with a pleasant climate especially in elevated areas.
  • Humidity: Comparatively lower than coastal districts, generally ranging between 50% to 70%.
Economy and Industry

Granite Industry

Automobile Manufacturing

Krishnagiri has a predominantly agrarian economy, supplemented by strong industrial and trade activities. The district is renowned for its mango cultivation, granite exports, and emerging industrial estates, particularly in Hosur, which has become a major manufacturing hub. Its strategic location near Bengaluru enhances trade and industrial development, attracting investments in manufacturing, electronics, and textiles.

Detailed Overview:

Agriculture:

  • Krishnagiri is one of the leading mango-producing districts in India, known especially for its Alphonso and Totapuri varieties.
  • Other major crops include ragi, groundnut, paddy, maize, and vegetables.
  • The district supports floriculture and horticulture with government-supported cold storage and processing facilities.

Industry:

  • Granite Industry: Krishnagiri is a major center for granite extraction and export, contributing significantly to its economy.
  • Automotive and Electronics Manufacturing: Hosur, an industrial town in the district, is home to companies like Ashok Leyland, TVS Motors, and Titan.
  • Textile and Garment Sector: Small and medium enterprises in textile manufacturing and garment exports are growing, especially near Hosur.
  • Food Processing: Mango-based food processing units including pulp and juice manufacturing are prevalent, particularly during harvest seasons.

Services:

  • The services sector is emerging, with growth in retail, banking, logistics, and hospitality in urban pockets like Hosur and Krishnagiri town.
  • Educational institutions and healthcare centers are expanding, catering to the growing population and industrial workforce.

Key Industries:

  • Granite and Stone Exports
  • Automotive Manufacturing
  • Textile and Garments
  • Electronics and Precision Tools
  • Mango Processing and Agro-based Industries

Infrastructure:

  • National Highway 44 (North-South Corridor) passes through Krishnagiri, providing strong connectivity to Bengaluru and Salem.
  • Well-developed industrial estates in Hosur with access to power, water, and skilled labor.
  • Proximity to Bengaluru International Airport enhances export potential and logistics efficiency.

Future Development:

  • Expansion of SIPCOT industrial estates and proposed electronics manufacturing clusters in Hosur.
  • Planned development of logistics parks and cold storage facilities to support agri-exports.
  • Efforts to promote skill development and technical education for the growing industrial sector.
Culture and Tourism

Krishnagiri is a culturally rich district with deep historical roots and natural beauty. It is known for its ancient forts, temples, and scenic landscapes. The district celebrates Tamil traditions through local festivals, folk arts, and traditional crafts. Its proximity to hill stations, religious sites, and forested areas makes it an emerging destination for heritage and eco-tourism.

Cultural Highlights:

Religious Sites:

  • Krishnagiri Fort: A historic hill fort built during the Vijayanagara period, offering panoramic views of the town.
  • Shree Parshwa Padmavathi Shaktipeet Tirth Dham: A major Jain pilgrimage site located in Krishnagiri town.
  • Hanumanthathirtham: A sacred site along the Ponnaiyar River, associated with legends of Lord Rama and Hanuman.

Historical Influence:

  • Ruled by various dynasties including the Cheras, Cholas, Vijayanagara Empire, and Tipu Sultan, Krishnagiri's heritage is reflected in its forts and temples.

Local Traditions:

  • Folk arts such as Karagattam and Oyilattam are performed during festivals in rural areas.
  • The district is known for traditional stone carving and mango-based handicrafts during the summer harvest season.

Tourism Attractions:

  • Krishnagiri Dam: A popular picnic spot built across the Thenpennai River, surrounded by lush green hills.
  • Kelevarapalli Reservoir: Near Hosur, known for bird watching and scenic views, especially during the monsoon.
  • Rayakottai Fort: A historical fort once used by Tipu Sultan, ideal for trekking and history enthusiasts.
  • Thali Hills: Located near Denkanikottai, a serene hill station with pleasant weather and tribal hamlets.
  • Kelamangalam Jain Temple: A peaceful spiritual site with intricate carvings and religious significance.
  • Hogenakkal Falls (nearby): Though in Dharmapuri district, it is a major attraction accessible from Krishnagiri for boat rides and scenic views.
Infrastructure and Development

Krishnagiri, located strategically along the Bengaluru–Chennai industrial corridor, is gradually developing its infrastructure with a focus on road connectivity, water management, and rural development. While primarily an agrarian district, recent years have seen investments in transportation, industrial clusters, and smart town planning to boost economic growth.

1. Infrastructure Development:

Smart Town Initiatives:

  • Efforts underway to implement smart town planning in Krishnagiri and Hosur, with digital services, improved sanitation, and LED street lighting.
  • Focus on creating cleaner, more efficient small towns through e-governance, waste segregation, and solar power systems.

Transportation:

  • NH-44 (part of the Golden Quadrilateral) runs through Krishnagiri, providing strong road connectivity to Bengaluru, Chennai, and Salem.
  • Widening and maintenance of state highways and rural roads under the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY) are ongoing.
  • Proposals for better bus terminals in Krishnagiri and Hosur with modern facilities.

Urban and Rural Infrastructure:

  • Development of rural drinking water supply schemes under Jal Jeevan Mission.
  • Projects for solid waste management and underground drainage in town panchayats.
  • Rural electrification and housing schemes have improved living standards in remote villages.

Industrial Infrastructure:

  • Hosur is a fast-growing industrial town with Special Economic Zones (SEZs) and industrial estates developed by SIPCOT.
  • Presence of major industries in electronics, auto components, and textiles in Hosur region.
  • Support infrastructure like logistics parks and warehouses being promoted along the Bengaluru–Krishnagiri corridor.

2. Development Plans:

Krishnagiri District Development Scheme:

  • Plans to promote agro-processing units, cold storage facilities, and mango-based industries given the district's agricultural strength.
  • Expansion of skill development centers in partnership with industries to promote local employment.

Hosur Urban Expansion:

  • Hosur Municipality is expanding rapidly as a satellite town of Bengaluru, with urban development focused on housing, commercial spaces, and IT parks.

3. Key Projects & Initiatives:

  • Hosur Industrial Cluster: One of the key manufacturing hubs in Tamil Nadu with excellent connectivity and industrial policy support.
  • Jal Jeevan Mission: Provides piped drinking water to rural households across the district.
  • PMGSY and Rural Roads Program: Expands all-weather road connectivity to remote villages.
  • Solar Street Lighting: Promoted in several rural areas as part of green energy adoption.
  • Drip Irrigation Support: Promoted in dry regions to support sustainable agriculture.
Key Highlights
  • Krishnagiri is a key district in Tamil Nadu known for its strategic location on the Bengaluru–Chennai corridor.
  • Serves as the administrative headquarters of Krishnagiri district, covering an area of approximately 5,129 sq. km.
  • Major economic sectors: Agriculture (especially mango cultivation), granite mining, electronics, and automobile manufacturing (in Hosur).
  • Tourist attractions: Krishnagiri Dam, KRP Dam Park, Government Museum, and the historic Rayakottai Fort.
  • Strong connectivity via NH-44, NH-77, and NH-42, with Hosur serving as a major industrial and transport hub near Bengaluru.
  • Known as the "Mango Capital of India" due to extensive mango orchards and export-oriented processing industries.
